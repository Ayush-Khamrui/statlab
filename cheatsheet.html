9 Probability Laws

Mutually Exclusive

Cannot happen at the same time.

$P(A \cap B) = 0$

Independent

One event doesn't affect the other.

"With Replacement"
$P(A \cap B) = P(A)P(B)$
⚠️ Mutually exclusive events CANNOT be independent (unless prob is 0).

Addition Rule (Union)

$$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$$

Conditional Prob

$$P(B|A) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}$$

10 Total Probability & Bayes Theorem

Total Probability

Used to find the total probability of an event B that depends on mutually exclusive events $A_i$.

$$P(B) = \sum P(B|A_i)P(A_i)$$
Prior $P(A)$
Likelihood $P(B|A)$
Evidence $P(B)$
Posterior $P(A|B)$
$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}$$

Distribution Selection Guide

Bernoulli 1 Trial Success/Fail
Binomial $n$ Trials Success/Fail

Normal Continuous Data

Binomial

  • 1. Fixed $n$
  • 2. Independent trials
  • 3. Constant $p$
  • 4. Two outcomes (S/F)
$$P(x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x q^{n-x}$$
Mean: $np$ Var: $npq$

Poisson

Approx Binomial if:
$n$ is large, $p$ is small.
Let $\lambda = np$
$$P(x) = \frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^x}{x!}$$
Mean: $\lambda$ Var: $\lambda$

Normal

68% - 95% - 99.7%

🚨 Exam Traps — Do Not Forget!

1. Mean is affected by outliers, Median is NOT.
2. Mutually Exclusive $\neq$ Independent. They are almost opposites.
3. Bayes theorem ALWAYS needs conditional probabilities to work.
4. For Poisson approximation to Binomial, use $\lambda = np$.
5. CLT guarantees a Normal sampling distribution only if $n \ge 30$.
6. If Confidence Level goes UP, the Interval Width gets WIDER.
